“Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare and Medicine: Clinical Applications, Therapeutic Advances, and Future Perspectives”

Fahim, Y. A., et al. “Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare and Medicine: Clinical Applications, Therapeutic Advances, and Future Perspectives.” European Journal of Medical Research, vol. 30, no. 848, 2025, Springer, https://doi.org/10.1186/s40001-025-03196-. Accessed 14 May 2026.

This article discusses the ways in which AI is currently being implemented within the healthcare system and its potential impact on a global scale. Access to healthcare is limited to middle and low income areas, especially in areas where the ratio between patients and doctors are extremely low. AI’s ability to analyze data can be transformative for diagnoses, surgeries, and medicine research. One notable and relevant finding in the article was that AI demonstrated it could predict influenza epidemics with 85% accuracy by analyzing social media and weather patterns. The authors of this article all have a strong background in medicine sciences and biology. The authors maintain that a responsible integration of AI in healthcare can not only reduce errors, but improve equity and access to medicine worldwide.

“Generative AI in Healthcare: Current Trends and Future Outlook 2025”

Pardo Martin, Carlos, et al. “Generative AI in Healthcare: Current Trends and Future Outlook 2025.” McKinsey & Company, 26 Mar. 2025, https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/healthcare/our-insights/generative-ai-in-healthcare-current-trends-and-future-outlook-2025. Accessed 14 May 2026.

This article examines the increasing adoption of generative AI within the healthcare industry and how they are being utilized into everyday operations. The work provided survey data from healthcare professionals on their current implementation processes/strategies of AI technologies. One survey found that 85% of healthcare organizations were already using or exploring generative AI systems as of 2025. The article’s use of survey driven data allows for more relevant and current statistics on the use of AI in healthcare. Written and funded by McKinsey & Company, this consulting firm focuses on providing research on technology, medicine, and business trends. They conclude in their article that organizations are transitioning from experimental to large-scale processes with a focus in administrative work, patient communications, and clinical routines. A core finding in this article that I found important was that AI’s integration into healthcare is not an incoming or speculative prediction, but rather is a current and expanding implementation supported by a growing industry of gen AI technologies specifically designed for medicine.

"AI Index Report 2025”

“AI Index Report 2025.” arXiv, Stanford Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence, 2025, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.07139. Accessed 14 May 2026.

The Stanford AI Index report analyzes the widespread use of AI, its growing trends on a global scale, and its use in research. The report provides statistics and evidence of AI’s expanding use and role in healthcare and medicine. One notable piece of evidence was the FDA’s approval of 223 AI medical devices as of 2023, compared to only 6 in 2015. The report includes relevant graphs, charts and quantitative data to show AI’s growth in numerous industries worldwide. Conducted and written by the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence, this institution is recognized for their work and research in AI ethics, policy, and advancement.
